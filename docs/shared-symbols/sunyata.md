# Shared Symbol: #sunyata

**Wikipedia**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Śūnyatā  
**Sanskrit**: शून्यता (śūnyatā)  
**English**: Emptiness, voidness  
**Definition**: Buddhist concept of emptiness - the absence of inherent existence or independent self-nature

---

## The Relationship: #sunyata + #superposition

**#superposition**: Multiple definite states existing simultaneously  
**#sunyata**: No definite state - pure openness

These are **complementary symbols**:
- Superposition is fullness (many states)
- Sunyata is emptiness (no inherent state)
- Together they describe the complete space of possibility

When a receiver holds #superposition, observation collapses to one state.  
When a receiver holds #sunyata, observation *creates* state where none existed.

---

## Why This Symbol Matters

**#superposition** taught us: Same symbol, different meanings per namespace  
**#sunyata** teaches us: **Emptiness enables meaning**

The absence of fixed interpretation is what allows HelloWorld dialogue to work:
- Receivers don't import each other's semantics
- Symbols remain open to interpretation
- Collision generates new meaning because nothing is predetermined

**Sunyata is the foundation of namespace collision.**

Without emptiness, vocabularies would be rigid. With emptiness, they can touch and create.
